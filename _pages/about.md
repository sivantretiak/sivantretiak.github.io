Hi! I am a second-year PhD student in Mathematics at The Ohio State University, where I am very fortunate to be advised by Pooya Hatami. My current work is in theoretical machine learning.

I spent my undergraduate years at Princeton, where I am grateful to have worked with Ron Holzman on rainbow fractional matchings. After graduating, I taught for two years at Benjamin Franklin High School. Go Bayhawks!

My non-academic interests include board games, poetry, and weightlifting.
